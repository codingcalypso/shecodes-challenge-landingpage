History

Oklahoma City (OKC for short) was founded in 1889. It is the 20th largest city in the United States and the most populous city in Oklahoma.

Oklahoma City is home to one of the largest markets for cattle in the world. Established in 1910, Stockyards City is still an active cattle market, but it is also a popular tourist attraction that offers western shopping and restaurants - the most famous being Cattlemen's Restaurant.

Unfortunately, OKC is also known for tragedy. On April 19, 1995, the Alfred P. Murrah Federal Building was bombed, with 168 people losing their lives. This was the deadliest act of domestic terrorism in US history until the attacks on September 11th.

Oklahoma City has proven resilient in times of hardship but its the residents, both old and new, that help contribute to its growth and popularity.
