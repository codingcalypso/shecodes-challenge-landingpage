History

The history of Oklahoma begins with this land being the home of many Native American tribes, many of whom were forcibly relocated to Oklahoma. In the late 1800s, the government decided to put the land up for settlement. Some individuals crossed the border "sooner" than they were allowed to, thus starting the nickname of Oklahoma as the "Sooner" State. Oklahoma officially became a state in 1907.

Quick Facts:

  • State Nickname: The Sooner State
  • State Bird: Scissor-tailed Flycatcher
  • State Flower: Oklahoma Rose
